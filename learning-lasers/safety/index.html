Chapter 6

Safety & Ventilation

Laser classes, optical density ratings for eyewear, fume extraction requirements by material, and enclosure guidelines for fibre galvo systems operating in a shared space.

Topics this chapter will cover

  • 👁️
    Laser classes and eye hazard — fibre lasers are Class 4 (>500 mW). 1064 nm IR is invisible to the human eye and passes through the cornea directly to the retina. A single reflected pulse can cause permanent blindness.
  • 🥽
    Optical density (OD) ratings — safety glasses must be rated for 1064 nm with OD ≥ 5 for Class 4 fibre lasers. Visible-light goggles provide zero protection. Always verify the wavelength printed on the glasses, not just the colour.
  • 💨
    Fume extraction — metal oxide fumes from steel are a nuisance but relatively benign at low concentrations. Wood, plastic, and coated surfaces require active extraction with a HEPA + activated carbon filter. Minimum: 150 m³/h extraction at the work surface.
  • 🏠
    Enclosures — the F2's built-in enclosure is an interlock-equipped Class 1 product when the lid is closed, making it safe for open-plan use. Using it with the lid open immediately reclassifies it as a Class 4 environment requiring full PPE.
  • 🔥
    Fire risk — never leave an active engrave unattended. Flammable materials (wood, leather, paper) can ignite if the beam dwells too long. Keep a CO₂ extinguisher within reach; water can damage electrical components.
  • 🌡️
    Ambient temperature and optics — galvo mirrors and the f-theta lens can accumulate surface contamination. Operating in high-humidity or dusty environments accelerates degradation. Keep the enclosure closed when not in use.
  • 📋
    Regulatory overview — UK/EU: BS EN 60825-1, CE marking. US: 21 CFR 1040.10. Hobby use at home generally falls under personal responsibility; commercial or shared-space use may require a written Laser Safety Officer designation.
