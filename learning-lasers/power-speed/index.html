Power & Speed

Energy density (J/mm²) as the master parameter — how power percentage, feed rate, and line spacing combine to control how much heat the laser deposits per unit area.

Topics this chapter will cover

  • ⚡
    Energy density formula — E = P / (v × s) where P is watts, v is speed in mm/s, and s is line spacing in mm. The single number that predicts colour and depth.
  • 📊
    Power % vs actual watts — how laser firmware maps 0–100% to real wattage, why the relationship is often non-linear at low values, and how to find your machine's effective minimum.
  • 🏃
    Speed and dwell time — slower speed increases dwell time quadratically in terms of heat, not linearly. The diminishing returns of going very slow.
  • 📏
    Lines per centimetre — how LPC sets hatch spacing, and why doubling LPC from 500 to 1000 doubles energy density before any other parameter changes.
  • 🌡️
    Heat accumulation vs heat dissipation — at very slow speeds or high LPC, heat builds faster than the material can dissipate it, leading to runaway temperature spikes at turn-around points.
