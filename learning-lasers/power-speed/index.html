Power & Speed

The two fundamental parameters that control every mark you make. Understanding their relationship is key to predicting results and diagnosing issues.

Every laser operation is defined by how much energy you deliver to a specific point on the material. While software gives you separate controls for Power and Speed, they are two sides of the same coin: Energy Density.

The Physics

The Formula: Energy Density ($E$) is Power ($P$) divided by the product of Speed ($S$) and Spot Size ($w$).
E = P / (S × w)

For a given machine, the spot size is usually fixed by the lens and beam quality. This leaves Power and Speed as your primary levers:

  • Double the Power: You double the energy delivered to each square millimetre.
  • Double the Speed: You halve the energy delivered, as the beam spends only half as much time over any given point.

The Tradeoff

There is rarely just one "correct" setting. You can often achieve the same energy density (and thus the same visual result) using different combinations.

Pro Tip: If you need to finish a job faster, try increasing both power and speed proportionally. As long as the ratio stays the same, the mark will look similar, but the laser head will move faster.

However, there are limits. Extremely high speed might outrun the laser's pulsing frequency (leading to "dotted" lines), while extremely low power might not reach the "threshold" temperature required for the material to react at all.

Key Takeaways

Goal: Deeper / Darker Increase Power OR Decrease Speed
Goal: Lighter / Surface Only Decrease Power OR Increase Speed
Goal: Faster Completion Increase Power AND Increase Speed

Energy Density Derivation

Note: Due to the microscopic 0.03mm beam size, these oxidation colors are typically only visible under magnification or when many lines are "hatched" together.

Step 1 Laser Power J/s
Formula: P = MaxW × %
5 W × 50 % = 2.500 J/s
Step 2 Beam Spot mm
Fixed focus width (w).
w = 0.03 mm
Step 3 Area Rate mm²/s
Formula: A = Speed × w
500 mm/s × 0.03 mm = 15.00 mm²/s
Step 4 Density J/mm²
Formula: E_raw = P / A
2.500 J/s / 15.00 mm²/s = 0.1667 J/mm²
Step 5 Scaling mJ/mm²
Formula: E = E_raw × 1000
0.1667 J/mm² × 1000 mJ/J = 166.7 mJ/mm²
