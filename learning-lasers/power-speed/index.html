Chapter 2

Power & Speed

The two fundamental parameters that control every mark you make. Understanding their relationship is key to predicting results and diagnosing issues.

Power (%) Speed (mm/s) Energy Density The Tradeoff

Power (%)

Power is the rate at which the laser emits energy, measured in Watts. In software, this is controlled by a percentage of the machine's maximum rated power.

Higher power delivers more energy per unit time to the material. This can lead to deeper engraving, faster cutting, or hotter temperatures for marking stainless steel.

Speed (mm/s)

Speed is how fast the laser head (or in a galvo system, the mirror's focal point) moves across the material surface.

Higher speed means the laser beam spends less time at any given point, reducing the total energy delivered to that spot. This results in lighter marks and faster job completion times.

Energy Density

Neither power nor speed alone determines the result. The crucial factor is energy density — the amount of energy delivered per unit area.

Think of it like a hose spraying water. Power is how much water is flowing out, and speed is how fast you move the nozzle. To get something really wet, you need high flow and slow movement. It's the same for lasers.

Energy Density ∝ Power / Speed

This is the fundamental relationship. If you double the power, you double the energy density. If you double the speed, you halve the energy density.

The Tradeoff

Every laser job is a balance between power, speed, and the desired outcome. For a given material, there is a sweet spot for energy density to achieve a certain effect (e.g., a dark anneal on steel, a deep cut in wood).

If your mark is too light, you can either increase power or decrease speed.

If your mark is too dark or burning the material, you can either decrease power or increase speed.

This tradeoff is why you'll often find multiple combinations of power and speed that produce similar results. The choice between them often comes down to job time: higher power and higher speed will finish faster than lower power and lower speed, even if the energy density is the same.
