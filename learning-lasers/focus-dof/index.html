Chapter 3

Focus & Depth of Field

Working distance calibration, the Rayleigh range as a practical depth-of-field limit, and deliberate defocus as a technique for spreading energy across thicker stock.

Coming soon

Topics this chapter will cover

  • 🎯
    What "focus" actually sets — on the F2 the focal plane is fixed by the lens; "focus offset" in software shifts the material height relative to the optimal focal distance to deliberately defocus.
  • 📐
    Calibrating working distance — the focus test card method, ramp tests, and why a 0.1 mm error in height doubles the spot area on a tight-focus lens.
  • 🔭
    Depth of field (2×z_R) — materials thinner than the depth of field can be marked with consistent quality in a single pass. Thicker materials require strategies like split-focus or stepped passes.
  • 🌀
    Deliberate defocus — moving the focus above or below the surface spreads the spot, reducing peak power density. Used to achieve softer marks, wider oxidation zones on steel, or less material removal on delicate stock.
  • 📦
    3D objects and curved surfaces — height variation across a curved object means parts of the design are out of focus. Strategies: rotary attachment, auto-focus, or accepting spot-size variation.
  • 🔬
    Interactive focus simulator — set material height offset and see predicted spot diameter, energy density, and expected colour result at the surface.
